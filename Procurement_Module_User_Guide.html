01 What Is the Procurement Pipeline?

The Procurement Pipeline is a Kanban-style tracking board where each procurement package moves through six stages from left to right:

Draft
▶
Submitted
▶
Advertised
▶
Evaluation
▶
No Objection
▶
Awarded

Each package represents a single procurement activity (e.g., "Diesel Fuel Purchase for GPL" or "Terminal Renovation Contract for CJIA"). The system tracks how long each package has been at its current stage, flags stalled items, and maintains a permanent audit trail of every action taken.

02 Accessing the Module

  1. Log in to Work OS with your Google Workspace account.
  2. Click Procurement in the left sidebar (Shopping Cart icon).

You will land on the Pipeline tab, which shows the Kanban board.

03 Understanding the Kanban Board

The Six Stages

StageWhat It Means
DraftPackage is being prepared. Not yet submitted for review.
SubmittedPackage is complete and ready for the procurement process to begin.
AdvertisedThe tender has been publicly posted and is open for bids.
EvaluationBids have been received and are being assessed.
No ObjectionAwaiting cabinet clearance before the contract can be awarded.
AwardedContract has been granted. This is the final stage.

Packages can only move forward — they cannot go back to a previous stage.

Reading a Card

Each card on the board displays:

  • Title of the procurement package
  • Agency badge (GPL, GWI, CJIA, or GCAA)
  • Estimated value in GYD
  • Procurement method (Open Tender, Selective Tender, Sole Source, or RFQ)
  • Days at current stage — color-coded:
    • Green = Less than 14 days (on track)
    • Amber = 14–29 days (approaching delay)
    • Red = 30+ days (stalled — needs attention)

Filtering & Stats

Use the filter chips at the top to view packages for a specific agency or select All. The stats bar shows four summary numbers: Active Packages, Pipeline Value, Avg Days to Award, and Stalled count.

04 Creating a New Package

Who can do this: Agency Admins only.
  1. Click the "+ New Package" button in the top-right corner.
  2. Fill in the form:
    • Title — A clear name for the procurement (e.g., "Generator Parts – Canefield Station")
    • Description — Details about what is being procured and why
    • Agency — The agency this package belongs to
    • Estimated Value — The budget for this procurement in GYD
    • Procurement Method — Choose one of four options (see below)
    • Expected Delivery Date (optional) — Target completion date
  3. Click Create Package.

The package will appear in the Draft column.

Procurement Methods

MethodDescriptionWhen to Use
Open Tender Public competitive bidding Standard procurement; maximum transparency
Selective Tender Limited to pre-qualified suppliers Specialized goods/services requiring vetted vendors
Sole Source Single supplier, no competition Emergencies or highly specialized needs
Request for Quotation Quotation-based process Lower-value purchases with known suppliers

05 Advancing a Package to the Next Stage

Who can do this: Agency Admins (own agency packages).

Option A: Drag and Drop

On a desktop computer, drag a card from one column and drop it into the next column. You cannot skip stages or move packages backward.

Option B: Detail Panel

  1. Click a card to open the detail panel.
  2. Click "Advance to [Next Stage]".
  3. Add optional notes (recommended).
  4. Confirm the advancement.

The stage change is recorded permanently in the package's history with your name and a timestamp.

06 Viewing Package Details

Click any card to open its Detail Panel on the right side. The panel contains four sections:

SectionWhat You'll Find
Stage Timeline A visual indicator showing where the package is in the pipeline, with completed stages highlighted.
Package Info Title, description, agency, estimated value, procurement method, who submitted it, expected delivery date.
Stage History A chronological log of every stage transition — from/to stage, who made the change, when, and any notes. This log is permanent and cannot be edited.
Documents All files attached to the package (contracts, bid documents, evaluation reports, etc.).
Notes An audit trail of comments from team members. Once posted, notes cannot be edited or removed.

07 Uploading Documents & Adding Notes

Uploading Documents

Who can do this: Agency Admins (own agency packages).
  1. Open the detail panel for a package.
  2. Scroll to the Documents section.
  3. Click Upload and select your file.
RequirementDetails
Supported formatsPDF, DOCX, XLSX, JPEG, PNG
Maximum file size10 MB per file

Documents are stored securely and organized by agency and package. To download a document, click its name in the documents list.

Adding Notes

Who can do this: All authenticated users (for packages within your agency).
  1. Open the detail panel for a package.
  2. Scroll to the Notes section.
  3. Type your note and click Send.

Notes are timestamped with your name and cannot be edited or deleted after posting. Use notes to record decisions, flag concerns, or provide context for stage transitions.

Tip: When advancing a package, always add a note explaining why. This builds a clear audit trail for compliance reviews.

09 Analytics — Reading the Charts

Where Are Things?

Stacked Bar Chart

Each bar represents a pipeline stage. Bars are color-segmented by agency (GPL, GWI, CJIA, GCAA), so you can see which agencies have the most packages at each stage.

How to read it: A tall bar at "Evaluation" means many packages are waiting for bids to be assessed. If one agency's colour dominates that bar, the bottleneck is specific to them.

How Long Is It Taking?

Horizontal Bar Chart

Each bar shows the average number of days packages spend at a given stage. Longer bars indicate slower stages in the process.

How to read it: If "No Objection" has the longest bar, it means cabinet clearance is the biggest time cost. Compare bars over time to see if process improvements are working.

What Is Stuck?

Alert Table

Lists every package that has been at its current stage for 30 or more days, sorted by estimated value (highest first). Each row shows the package title, agency, stage, days stuck, and value.

How to read it: This is your action list. High-value items at the top are your top priority. If the table is empty, the pipeline is healthy.

Pipeline Value

Summary Statistics

Shows the total GYD value of all active (non-awarded) packages, broken down by stage. Gives you a financial view of where procurement dollars are sitting in the pipeline.

How to read it: If the majority of pipeline value is concentrated in early stages, procurement is still ramping up. If it's concentrated at "No Objection," a large volume of spend is awaiting cabinet clearance.

Filtering applies to analytics too. If you select a specific agency using the filter chips on the Pipeline tab, the Analytics tab will also filter to show only that agency's data. Use this to prepare for agency-specific reviews.

10 Analytics — Taking Action on Insights

The Analytics tab is not just for reporting — it should drive your weekly workflow. Below are common patterns and what to do about them.

What You SeeWhat It MeansWhat to Do
Multiple packages stalled at "Evaluation" Bid assessments are backlogged — evaluation committees may be stretched or waiting on information. Follow up with the evaluation committee. Check if terms of reference are complete and if assessors need support.
"No Objection" bar is the longest Cabinet clearance is the slowest stage in the pipeline. Ensure submission packages are complete before advancing. Coordinate with the Cabinet Secretary's office on scheduling.
One agency has significantly more stalled packages That agency may have capacity issues or process gaps. Schedule a review with the agency's procurement officer. Check if they need training or additional resources.
Avg Days to Award is rising The overall procurement cycle is slowing down. Compare stage durations to find the bottleneck. Address the slowest stage first — it has the most impact on total cycle time.
High pipeline value at "Draft" Large procurement packages are being prepared but not yet submitted. Check if drafts are blocked by missing information or approvals. Reach out to the submitting officers to unblock.
Stalled table is empty No packages have been at the same stage for 30+ days. The pipeline is healthy. Continue monitoring weekly.
Recommended practice: Review the Analytics tab at the start of each week. Use the "What is stuck?" table as your meeting agenda for procurement follow-ups.

11 Mobile Usage & Key Rules

Mobile Usage

On mobile devices, the Kanban board automatically switches to a single-column view with a tab bar at the top to switch between stages. Drag-and-drop is not available on mobile — use the detail panel's Advance button instead.

All other features — viewing details, uploading documents, adding notes, and accessing analytics — work the same on mobile as on desktop.

Key Rules

1
Forward only. Packages can only advance to the next stage. They cannot be moved backward or skip stages.
2
Everything is recorded. Every stage change, note, and document upload is logged with your name and a timestamp.
3
Notes are permanent. Once you post a note, it cannot be edited or deleted. Double-check before submitting.
4
30-day stall threshold. Any package sitting at the same stage for 30+ days will be flagged red on the board and appear in the "What is stuck?" analytics table.
5
10 MB file limit. Keep uploaded documents under 10 MB. Accepted formats: PDF, DOCX, XLSX, JPEG, PNG.
